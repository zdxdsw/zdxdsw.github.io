Common mistakes in the first round of qualification are summarized below:

We don't allow:
  • "Fake multi-img question" which actually contains two separate questions and requires two separate answers.
  • "Fake multi-img question" where the two images are not complementary. This means actually a single image is necessary to get the answer. The question would simply boil down to a single-img one in this case.
  • Answer cannot be inferred from visual clues. The answer should be provided by the visual content rather than textual metadata on Wikipedia pages. Please see FAQ #6 for further details.
  • Question contains referential phrases that are img-specific. Here is a detailed clarification of this requirement: In our downstream task, we will let a model see the question first and then let it select images that may provide useful clues from a large image pool. Thus, the question should be understandable without any image pairing with it.
1. What type of flooring can be found at the prayer hall of the Great Mosque of Kairouan?

Is this a good question?

Bad

Images are not complementary. Only one image is enough to answer this question.
2. Do prayer halls of both the Great Mosque of Kairounan and the Badshahi Mosque use a flooring pattern with dark grid lines at their entrances?

Is this a good question?

Good

The images are complementary because they focus on different aspects.
3. Are there humanoid statues on the roof of St. Peter's Basilica in Vatican City and can a clock be found on the front exterior of Nadur Basilica?

Is this a good question?

Bad

This consists of two separate questions.
4. Can a clock be found on the vertical central axis of both St. Peter's Basilica in Vatican City and Nadur Basilica?

Is this a good question?

Good

One coherent question is able to link two images!
5.
During the Olympic Torch Relay held by which country was there a Coke Cola themed vehicle?

Is this a good question?

Bad

Answer cannot be inferred from the image. It is from the Wikipedia page that we know the answer should be the 2012 Olympic Torch Relay held by Britain.
6.
During the 2012 Olympic Torch Relay, where is the Olympic ring logo in front of the Coke Cola themed vehicle relative to a torch logo?

Is this a good question?

Good

The answer can be inferred from visual clues!
7.
Were all three men wearing hats of the same color?

Is this a good question?

Bad

"All three men" is a bad referential phrase. It is meaningless when no image is provided.
8.
Were all three men wearing hats of the same color when they were being photographed at the Mascot Ceremony at the Men's Giant Slalom in the 2020 Winter Youth Olympics?

Is this a good question?

Good

Explicitly specifying the location/time/event makes a lot more sense!

We do allow:
  • Open-domain questions with more than one correct answer. It is acceptable for a multi-img question that it has multiple correct answers while the images only provide clues to a subset of correct answers. If you find this confusing since we also require a question to have a 'definitive answer', please see FAQ #5 for further explanation.
  • Binary questions: Although we encourage more non-binary questions, binary questions are still acceptable if it meets all other requirements.
14. Which Olympic mascots would be predators if they were real animals?

Is this a good question?

Good

The number of correct answers might be more than two but this is acceptable because the images do provide clues to a subset of correct answers!
