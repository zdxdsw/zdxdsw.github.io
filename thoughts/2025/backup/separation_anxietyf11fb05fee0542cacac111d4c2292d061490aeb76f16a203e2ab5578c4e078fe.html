There are several differences this time, however, the first two on my side, the third one on my fear's side.
  1. I foresee it, which means I can prepare for it, starting now.

Perhaps there is also something to learn from my experience in dealing with separation from my parents. For example, those airport-related concepts no longer trigger pure sorrow or utter helplessness. They got overwritten with new memories about travel. Yep ‚Äî> always leverage my success in dealing with physical distance to deal with mental distance! Why can I solo-travel to a new place with no acquaintance and function well? Nobody cares about when my plane takes off, whether I get to the hotel before it's too late, or whether I enjoy the scenery. But I'm ok with it and actually enjoy it. I think this is a rather nice base case which I've already laid, firmly. I only need to work out the inductive case. I know there's a qualitative difference, though. That is, traveling doesn't shift your anchor. But starting a new life chapter requires changing your anchor, which feels much more dreadful. Especially, I feel like, this time nobody will be catching you at the new anchor. I have to either catch myself, or grow the ability to gracefully land.

Having all that said (Apr1 2025), I must start right now doing everything that can be a favor for the future me. Paramount above all, I must strive for a new anchor which makes me excited.

Today (Apr2 2025) as I was doing the cliff walk in Newport, I came up with a fourth coping strategy: internalizing Yonatan. Though I know I can never 100% accomplish this, nor can I even get close to 30%, it doesn't stop me from getting excited about applying this strategy because it will be the most powerful one in weakening the impact of separation ‚Äî if only I could take part of it with me wherever I go. In fact I've had this thought before but didn't take it very seriously. Nor did I flesh it out into a serious plan that calls for changes in my behavior. I guess I will do it this time then.
